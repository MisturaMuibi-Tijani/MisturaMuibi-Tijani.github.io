Abstract

Most students in underserved rural communities, if admitted to universities, are usually first-generation students and lack people they can look up to for mentoring. At this age, some students have never operated or seen a computer. There is a high level of poverty in these communities contributing to the low performance of high school students in standard examinations like UTME.

Most exams are now computer-based, and students perform poorly because they usually have little experience with computers. An average student in these communities aims for low-ranked higher institutions because of a lack of awareness about better alternatives.

Coming across this image on during the COVID lockdown got our founder thinking of ways to help the underserved. She set up a survey to get information on how rural students are learning during the lockdown. However, Only a few responded due to internet inaccessibility. Not satisfied, she distributed hard copies to complement the ones filled online.

The survey asked questions such as their name, level, village, whether they are enrolled in any education activity during the lockdown, what they know about online learning, and tested their knowledge on tech terms such as programming, cloud computing, AI, IoT, software engineering, etc. The average price the students indicated they could afford to pay monthly is ( ~ $3).

Introduction

VertSTEM is derived from two words "Vert" and "STEM". "Vert" means green in french and "STEM" is an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering, Math. The green color is the color of life, It also symbolizes fertility and freshness of the young minds. The organization is to enlighten young minds about technology trends and to help bridge the digital and the economic divide, inclusive of the underserved communities

The name has been reserved with CAC Nigeria and awareness has been published on the Daily Times Newspaper and Nigerian NewsDirect

The Idea

VertSTEM's vision is to make STEM education accessible to all. It aims to achieve this by having a structure that will be favourable to both the haves and the haves not. The foundation will set up facilities such as the Internet, computers, and dedicated workspace to help the underserved learn from resources online as they can not afford their computing resources.

A team of 10 pre-university students will be given 3 months of access to the dedicated workspace. They will have the opportunity to complete self-paced programming courses online and our founder will join them virtually monthly to motivate them using IEEE TryEngineering resources.

There will be a premium package for the haves which does not include setting up physical facilities, but that of the underserved individuals will be heavily discounted, such that the organization is sustainable.

Objectives

•Secondary schools outreach to encourage youngsters to pursue careers in STEM.

•To foster and expand the interest of female students in technological advancement.

•To empower pre-university students in making advances in STEM.

•To allow students to engage in hands-on engineering activities.

•To train teachers on how to engage students in STEM.

•To connect them with university alumni and professionals to mentor them.

•To encourage them to volunteer for the good of society.
